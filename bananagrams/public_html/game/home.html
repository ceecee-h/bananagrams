How to Play
The objective of Bananagrams is to be the first player to use all of their letters.
Each player will start with 21 tiles. When the game begins, each player races to arrange their tiles in their own intersecting word grid... Words must read left to right horizontally, or top to bottom vertically. Players may rearrange their grid at any time, and as often as they like.
If a player arranges all of their tiles in an interconnected grid, they can “peel,” and each player will receive a new tile they must add to their grid.
If a player has a tile that they don't want to use, they can “dump,” and return that tile to the pool and get 3 new ones. Players cannot dump if there are less than 3 tiles in the pool.
When there are less tiles than players remaining in the pool, a player can call “BANANAS!”. If their tiles are in a fully interconnected grid and all of the words they have formed (horizontally and vertically, NOT diagonally) are valid, they win the game. But: be careful, if a player calls BANANAS and their board is not connected or contains invalid words, that player will lose the game, and all other players will be credited with a win.
